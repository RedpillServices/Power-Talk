Losers

Losers are the dispossessed people in society who know they are dispossessed.

An example of Losers is a group of coworkers at a large regional company. They have no real power over the day to day operations of their company, and thus a significant portion of their lives.

Losers are people who realize that this is a raw deal and respond in kind - they get meaning through outer activities - hobbies, social life and clubs.

To Losers, social life/belonging is the most important aspect of life.

Clueless

Clueless are the dispossessed people in society who don't know they are dispossessed. In other words, "useful idiots".

The Clueless are frequently company men - middle managers and others who believe in organizations, but who don't benefit the most from them.

Clueless individuals tend to draw most of their meaning in life from the formal organizations (such as a company) in their lives.

They are typically over-achievers.

Sociopath

Sociopaths are people who want to understand and have as much control as possible over reality.

Sociopaths try to extract understanding and value. They are frequently CEOs and executives.

Sociopaths frequently use Powertalk to get what they want and to say something without saying it directly.

They are are oriented towards winning.

Straight Talk

Straight Talk is about directly conveying information in an easily understandable manner. Everyone uses Straight Talk at some point or another.

Example 1:

Two roommates are at their apartment. One of them says to the other:

"Hey, do you want to get ice cream?"

There is no social status at stake here. There is no argument, hidden motive or any other reason. One of the roommates just wants to know if the other one wants ice cream.

Straight Talk

Powertalk is frequently taken for Straight Talk.

Example 3:

A group of five guys is sitting around. Guy 1 says something awkward and another guy responds:

Guy 2 - "What is wrong with you?"

Guy 1 - "I have ADD."

The actual meaning of this phrase is something along the lines of, "shut up" or "you're awkward". Guy 1 is interpreting it as Straight Talk. Literally, why are you acting the way you are?

This shows a lack of social competence.

Powertalk

Powertalk is the art of saying something without directly saying it.

Example 1:

A senior executive says to a junior executive:

"If you want to advance, you need to cut labor costs in your department."

On its face, this means exactly what it looks like it means - labor costs are high and need to be reduced. In reality, there's the hidden subtext that the only real way to reduce labor costs is to get people to work for less pay, or more likely lay people off.

Powertalk

Powertalk is frequently used in relationship disputes.

Example 2:

A guy asks his girlfriend what's wrong, she responds with:

"Nothing."

This is a well known dating trope and it has been a huge source for comedy and confusion. What does nothing mean in this context? It has several meanings.

Meaning #1: Something is wrong and I want you to be able to discern that without having to tell you directly.

Meaning #2: If you aren't able to discern it, it possibly means you don't care about me as much as I thought you did.

Meaning #3: I don't want to talk about it. I want you to fix it.

The astute observer can probably think of several more. Some people (guys mostly) might say the woman is lying in this situation. That is wrong. She's just using Powertalk.

Women achieve verbal fluency earlier than men do, and that may suggest that the average woman naturally understands Powertalk to a greater extent, naturally, than the average man does.

Powertalk

Straight Talk can be confused for Powertalk

Example 3:

Let's go back to the guy and his girlfriend in Example 2, about a half hour earlier:

"Hey, want to come to the gym with me tomorrow? I think it'd be good for you."

This is a sentence that can be interpreted in both Powertalk and Straight Talk. The boyfriend in this scenario simply thinks he's trying to get his girlfriend to live a healthier life. There's no ulterior motive.

However, he lacks the tact to know that sometimes people interpret Straight Talk as Powertalk. In this instance, the girlfriend might interpret his statement as, "Wow, you're looking really out of shape lately." or even, "If you don't go to the gym, I'll leave you."

Gametalk

Gametalk is used to mutually make people feel better about their losses

Example 1:

Two teenage boys try out for the football team. They don't make it:

Guy 1 - "Well it doesn't really matter anyway. We have a lot of other things we can do."

Guy 2 - "Yeah! I bet we're better than all of them at smoking people at Battlefield Duty 2252!"

These two have decided to massage their bruised egos by making each other feel better about not succeeding. The situation has been changed from a lack of success, to a positive situation.

This is pure rationalization to avoid ego bruising.

Savvy people might recognize that girl 2 is most likely wrong about her analysis, and Chad in question does not intend to commit.
